Inventory Audit Report: Sector Q4 Facility

The Sector Q4 storage facility is divided into four color-coded zones, each housing a specific mix of boxed shipments and high-value loose commodities. Starting with the Black Room, which acts as the facility's high-security vault, we find three primary container types. The most valuable is the Pink Box, appraised at $4,000, followed by the Green Box at $1,200, and the more economical Blue Box at $340. Beyond these containers, the room holds loose luxury items including Artisan Dark Chocolates valued at $85 per gold-leaf tray, Vintage Fountain Pens at $1,250 per mahogany case, and individual specimens of Organic Dragon Fruit priced at $12 each.
Moving into the climate-controlled Blue Room, the inventory shifts toward perishables and hardware. Here, Yellow Boxes are valued at $2,100 while Grey Boxes stand at $450. The loose inventory is equally diverse; Premium Truffles are stored in vacuum-sealed pouches worth $300, while Gen 9 Micro-Processors are handled in silicon trays valued at $890. This room also contains crates of Iced Lychee Fruit, which carry a valuation of $45.
The Pink Room serves as the hub for textiles and confectionery goods. The container hierarchy here consists of Purple Boxes at $600 and White Boxes at $150. Notable loose items include burlap sacks of Ruby Cocoa Beans valued at $210, designer boxes of Silk Scarves priced at $120, and Alphonso Mangoes, which are sold at $65 per dozen.
Finally, the Orange Room spans the largest square footage of the facility, accommodating heavy equipment and rare spices. It houses the facility's most expensive container, the Black Box, valued at $9,500, alongside the Red Box at $3,200. The loose items in this sector are highly specialized: small glass vials of Saffron Filaments are valued at $1,100, industrial packs of Titanium Bolts at $15, and 500g units of Manuka Honey Jars at $95.
